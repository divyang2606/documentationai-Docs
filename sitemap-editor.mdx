The Sitemap Editor is a visual canvas where you plan your website's pages and sections. SproutOS generates the initial structure from your Guided Brief. You then add, remove, reorder, or rename anything before moving to design. Every structural change you make here updates your design automatically.

## Understanding the Canvas

The sitemap displays your website as a set of page nodes. Each node contains the sections assigned to that page. You can expand or collapse any node to see its sections.

Pages are organized into three containers:

| Container | What Goes Here |
|---|---|
| **Static Pages** | Main website pages: Home, About, Services, Contact, etc. |
| **Blog Pages** | Blog index, post templates, category and tag pages |
| **Legal Pages** | Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Cookie Policy |

## Managing Pages

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a page" icon="plus">
    Click **Add Page** to create a new page. Give it a name and it appears as a new node on the canvas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rename a page" icon="edit">
    Click the page name directly on the node to rename it inline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reorder pages" icon="move">
    Drag any page node to a new position on the canvas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Duplicate a page" icon="copy">
    Right-click a page node and select **Duplicate**. This copies the page along with all its sections.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete a page" icon="trash">
    Right-click a page node and select **Delete**. SproutOS asks you to confirm before removing it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Sections

Sections sit inside each page node. You can add, reorder, copy, and delete sections at any time.

- **Add a section** — open a page node and click **Add Section** to choose from the section library
- **Reorder sections** — drag sections up or down within a page
- **Copy a section** — right-click a section to copy it, then paste it into any other page
- **Delete a section** — click the delete icon on any section

### Global Sections

Mark a section as **Global** to share it across all pages automatically. Use this for your Navbar and Footer. Editing a global section in the Design Editor updates it on every page at once.

## Section Types

SproutOS includes 51+ section types. Here are the most common categories:

| Category | Section Types |
|---|---|
| Navigation | Navbar, Mega Menu, Header |
| Content | Hero, About Us, Services, Features, Metrics |
| Social Proof | Testimonials, Company Logos, Portfolio, Gallery |
| Conversion | CTA, Contact Form, Pricing Table |
| Information | FAQ, Blog, Team, Location |
| Structure | Footer |

## Using the AI Chat

The AI chat is built into the Sitemap Editor. Describe what you want and SproutOS applies the changes directly to your sitemap.

Examples:

- "Add a pricing page with a comparison table and FAQ section"
- "Remove the blog and add a case studies page instead"
- "Add a testimonials section to every service page"

<Callout kind="tip">
  Use the AI chat when making multiple changes at once. It is faster than editing nodes manually for large structural changes.
</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Expandable title="Does changing the sitemap affect my design?">
  Adding or removing sections updates your design automatically. Reordering sections changes the order in the Design Editor. Existing design customizations on unchanged sections are preserved.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What are global sections?">
  Global sections are shared across all pages. Typically these are your Navbar and Footer. Editing a global section in the Design Editor updates it everywhere at once.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Can I add section types that are not in the library?">
  No. You can only use section types available in the library. Use the AI chat to combine existing sections creatively if you need something specific.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Can I undo changes I make to the sitemap?">
  Yes. Use Ctrl+Z to undo recent manual changes. Changes applied by the AI chat also have an undo option.
</Expandable>
