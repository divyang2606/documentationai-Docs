<Callout kind="info">
  **Phase 4 of 4:** Brief → Sitemap → Design → You are here: **Export**. Your design is ready. Export sends it directly to your platform of choice.
</Callout>

Once your design is ready in the Design Studio, export it to WordPress or Figma with a few clicks. SproutOS packages your entire project (pages, sections, content, and styles) and makes it available for import inside your chosen platform via the SproutOS plugin.

## Supported Export Platforms

| Platform | What You Get |
|----------|-------------|
| **Elementor** | Full page layouts ready to edit in Elementor |
| **Gutenberg** | Block-based layouts for the WordPress block editor |
| **Figma** | Design file importable into your Figma workspace |

## Step 1: Export From SproutOS

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the export menu" icon="download">
    From the Design Studio, click the **Export** dropdown in the top-right corner of the screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your platform" icon="layers">
    Select your target platform: **Elementor**, **Gutenberg**, or **Figma**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the export" icon="check">
    A confirmation dialog appears. Review the details and click **Export** to package your project. SproutOS prepares your export and makes it available for import.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 2: Import Into Your Platform

The import process works the same way across all three platforms. Install the SproutOS plugin, log in, and your exported project appears automatically.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Elementor" icon="layout">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Install the SproutOS plugin" icon="plug">
        In your WordPress admin, go to **Plugins > Add New** and search for **SproutOS**. Install and activate the plugin.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Log in to SproutOS" icon="log-in">
        Open the SproutOS plugin from your WordPress sidebar. Log in with the same credentials you use on the SproutOS Dashboard.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Import your project" icon="download">
        Your exported project appears as a card in the plugin. Click **Import** and SproutOS loads your pages and sections directly into Elementor.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Gutenberg" icon="grid">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Install the SproutOS plugin" icon="plug">
        In your WordPress admin, go to **Plugins > Add New** and search for **SproutOS**. Install and activate the plugin.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Log in to SproutOS" icon="log-in">
        Open the SproutOS plugin from your WordPress sidebar. Log in with the same credentials you use on the SproutOS Dashboard.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Import your project" icon="download">
        Your exported project appears as a card in the plugin. Click **Import** and SproutOS loads your design as Gutenberg blocks, ready to edit.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Figma" icon="figma">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Install the SproutOS Figma plugin" icon="plug">
        Open Figma and go to **Plugins > Browse Plugins**. Search for **SproutOS** and install the plugin.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Log in to SproutOS" icon="log-in">
        Open the SproutOS plugin inside Figma. Log in with the same credentials you use on the SproutOS Dashboard.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Import your project" icon="download">
        Your exported project appears as a card in the plugin. Click **Import** and SproutOS loads your design into the Figma canvas.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout kind="tip">
  Use the same email and password you use to log in at [dashboard.sproutos.ai](https://dashboard.sproutos.ai). The plugin uses the same account to match your exported projects.
</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Expandable title="Can I export the same project to multiple platforms?">
  Yes. You can export any project to Elementor, Gutenberg, and Figma independently. Each export is separate and does not affect the others.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Does the import overwrite my existing WordPress pages?">
  No. SproutOS creates new pages on import. Your existing WordPress content is not modified.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Can I re-export after making changes in the Design Studio?">
  Yes. Go back to the Design Studio, make your changes, and export again. Click Import in the plugin to pull in the updated version.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="The plugin shows no projects after logging in. What should I check?">
  Make sure you exported the project from the Design Studio first. The export step is required before the project appears in the plugin. If you exported and it still does not appear, log out of the plugin and log back in to refresh.
</Expandable>
