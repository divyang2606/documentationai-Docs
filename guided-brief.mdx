The Guided Brief is a 6-step discovery wizard that captures everything about your client's project before any design begins. You answer questions about the business, audience, brand, and references. SproutOS uses your answers to generate a tailored sitemap and design. The more detail you provide, the more accurate the output.

## Before You Start

<Callout kind="info">
  - A project created from the dashboard
  - Basic information about the client's business, goals, and brand
</Callout>

## The 6 Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Project Basics" icon="info">
    Enter the project name, website type, and primary goals.

    - **Website type:** Business, E-Commerce, Portfolio, Blog, Digital Agency, SaaS
    - **Primary goals:** Leads, Bookings, Calls, Sales, Sign-ups, Visits
    - **Target locations:** Local, National, Global

    If the client has an existing website, paste the URL here. SproutOS crawls it and extracts brand insights automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Business Snapshot" icon="briefcase">
    Describe the business so SproutOS understands what it offers and who it serves.

    - Top offers and value propositions
    - Primary customer description
    - Unique differentiators
    - Preferred tone: Premium, Friendly, Bold, Minimal, Playful, Corporate
    - Tones to avoid
  </Step>

  <Step title="Copy and Trust Building" icon="shield">
    Add proof elements that build trust with website visitors.

    - Customer objections or hesitations to address
    - Available proof: Testimonials, Case Studies, Results, Certifications
    - Supporting documents or links
  </Step>

  <Step title="Brand Direction" icon="palette">
    Define the visual identity of the project.

    - Brand style: Minimal, Premium, Playful, Bold, Corporate, Elegant, Techy, Organic
    - Logo upload
    - Preferred colors (hex input)
    - Colors to avoid
    - Typography preferences
  </Step>

  <Step title="References" icon="link">
    Add websites the client likes and dislikes. SproutOS crawls each URL and extracts colors, fonts, navigation structure, and content tone automatically.

    - **Liked sites** — URL and what you like about it (colors, layout, typography, feel)
    - **Disliked sites** — what to avoid and why
  </Step>

  <Step title="Improve Accuracy" icon="target">
    Add final context to sharpen the AI output.

    - Competitor URLs
    - Preferred sections to include in the website
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What SproutOS Does With Your Brief

Once you complete the brief, SproutOS analyzes everything you entered and generates a full sitemap. The AI uses your business description, brand direction, tone preferences, and reference sites to build a page structure matched to the project.

<Callout kind="tip">
  You can always edit the sitemap after it is generated. The brief sets the starting point. Nothing is locked in.
</Callout>

## URL Crawling

When you paste a website URL anywhere in the brief, SproutOS crawls it automatically and extracts:

- Color palette
- Typography (heading and body fonts)
- Navigation structure
- Contact information and social links
- Testimonials and CTAs
- Brand tone and style

This works for the client's existing site, reference sites they like, and competitor URLs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Expandable title="Do I have to complete all 6 steps?">
  No. You can skip steps and generate a sitemap with partial information. More detail produces a more accurate result, but a basic project description in Step 1 is enough to get started.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Can I edit the brief after generating the sitemap?">
  Yes. You can return to the brief at any time and update it. Changes to the brief do not automatically regenerate the sitemap. You would need to regenerate manually.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What if I don't have all the client information yet?">
  Fill in the fields you have and leave the rest blank. You can return to the brief later as you gather more information from the client.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Can I share the brief with my client?">
  Yes. You can invite the client to the project with a Client role. They can view the brief and leave comments without accessing the design or export.
</Expandable>
