SproutOS works from plain language. You describe what you want - your AI figures out which abilities to call and in what order. These are real workflows you can run today.

## Site Maintenance

> "Check for plugin updates each morning, take a full UpdraftPlus backup first, then apply updates one by one and verify the site after each one."

> "Run a complete site health check: uptime, pending updates, database size, broken links, disk usage."

> "Find all posts without a featured image and flag them for review."

> "Find and delete all post revisions older than 30 days."

## Performance

> "Run a full Lighthouse audit and trace the exact cause of the slow score."

> "Check our autoload data and tell me what's slowing down the site."

> "Find abandoned post revisions, orphaned metadata, expired transients, and spam comments - report first, then clean up."

## Security

> "Scan our theme files for any hardcoded credentials."

> "Audit all active plugins and check for known vulnerabilities."

## Content and SEO

> "Create a landing page for our summer sale with this content."

> "List all WooCommerce orders from last month and show me the total revenue."

> "Find all hardcoded URLs in theme files before we move to the new domain."

## Code and Automation

> "Change the button border radius to 8px across the whole site."

> "Create and deploy a custom PHP snippet that adds a banner to all product pages."

> "Find and trace the JavaScript error caused by a plugin conflict, then fix it."

## WooCommerce

> "Export orders with custom filters in plain English."

> "List products with stock below 5 units."

> "Generate a coupon report for last quarter."

## How to Write Good Prompts

You don't need to know ability names or technical details. A few habits make prompts more effective:

- **Be specific about the outcome, not the method.** "Change the hero heading color to blue" works better than "edit style.css line 42."
- **Reference your actual setup.** Say "our active theme" or "the Elementor homepage" so the AI reads the right files first.
- **Ask for a report before asking for changes.** When exploring an unfamiliar part of your site, start with "show me" or "find" before "update" or "delete."
- **Break large tasks into steps.** If a task has many moving parts, give the AI one phase at a time and review between them.
